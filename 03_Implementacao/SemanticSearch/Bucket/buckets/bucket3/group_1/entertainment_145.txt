Richard and Judy choose top books  The 10 authors shortlisted for a Richard and Judy book award in 2005 are hoping for a boost in sales following the success of this years winner.  The TV couples interest in the book world coined the term "the Richard & Judy effect" and created the top two best-selling paperbacks of 2004 so far. The finalists for 2005 include Andrew Taylors The American Boy and Robbie Williams autobiography Feel. This years winner, Alice Sebolds The Lovely Bones, sold over one million. Joseph OConnors Star of the Sea came second and saw sales increase by 350%. The best read award, on Richard Madeley and Judy Finnigans Channel 4 show, is part of the British Book Awards. David Mitchells Booker-shortlisted novel, Cloud Atlas, makes it into this years top 10 along with several lesser known works.  "Theres no doubt that this years selection of book club entries is the best yet. If anything, the choice is even wider than last time," said Madeley. "It was very hard to follow last years extremely successful list, but we think this years books will do even better," said Richard and Judy executive producer Amanda Ross. "We were spoiled for choice and it was tough getting down to only 10 from the 301 submitted."
